Practical, online wellness training for teams — and for the line managers who notice something is wrong first. Built and led by an accredited wellness and HR specialist.

Why this, why now

Burnout is a business cost long before it's a crisis

It shows up as absence, as good people leaving, as work quietly taking twice as long. By the time it's obvious, it's expensive — and the person is already exhausted.

Most wellness training fails because it asks people to add something to a day that's already full. This programme does the opposite: small habits that fit inside the work, and managers equipped to spot trouble while spotting it still helps.

Straight about what it is: this is professional development, not a compliance exercise. It earns a certificate, not scorecard points — and it's worth doing for what it changes at work.

Being clear

What this is, and what it isn't

This is education, not treatment. It builds understanding, habits and better conversations at work. It is not therapy, not a diagnosis, and not a substitute for professional mental health care — and the manager track is explicit that a manager's job is to notice and signpost, never to play therapist.

If you or someone you work with needs real support, speak to your GP, your employer's employee assistance programme, or a mental health professional. In South Africa, the South African Depression and Anxiety Group (SADAG) runs free counselling helplines — details at sadag.org.
