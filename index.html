A new model

Credit for credit

Bursaries are usually awarded on need, on paperwork, or on who you happen to know. We think the fairest signal is already sitting there: the credits you earned by doing the work.

1 · Bring your credits

The credits you've already acquired at school — and every module you complete here — count. You did the work; it shouldn't evaporate.

2 · They're given a value

Credits are assessed against the qualification pathway, so what you hold is turned into a number rather than a vague sense of merit.

3 · You get a credit refund

That value comes back to you as funding toward your studies. Do well, and the doing well is what pays — that's the whole idea in the name.

4 · Keep climbing

Put it toward the next module, and those credits stack too — onto a record that follows you, toward a national qualification you keep.

This is how the Credit Refund model works, and it's what we're building toward. Credit refunds are not open for application yet — tell us you want one and we'll come to you the moment they are. The modules and the credits are live today.

How it works

Modules → credits → a qualification you keep

Credit for Credit isn't a one-off course. Each module is a small, credit-bearing unit you can start in days — not months. Every module you complete carries credits toward the Occupational Certificate: Computer Technician (NQF 5, 282 credits).

Learn at your own pace, around your work. The credits accumulate on your record — so nothing you finish is ever wasted, and you decide how far up the ladder you go.

The destination

The accredited pathway

The Occupational Certificate: Computer Technician is the nationally accredited qualification the shorter modules ladder toward — delivered through an accredited Skills Development Provider, accredited by the Quality Council for Trades and Occupations (QCTO).

Choose your modules, build credits at your own pace, and earn a national credential that's yours to keep — portable across every employer.

Your record

Academic credit becomes financial credit

The credits don't just sit in a file somewhere. Every module you finish — and how well you did it — builds one verified record that belongs to you, not to the employer who paid for it.

That record is what you put in front of the next employer, the next opportunity, anyone deciding what you're worth — and it's what you apply with when you claim a credit refund. You choose who sees it, and you can withdraw that access at any time.
