How it works

Modules → credits → a qualification they keep

SPS Academy isn't a one-off course. Each module is a small, credit-bearing unit your people can start in days — not months. Every module they complete carries credits toward the Occupational Certificate: Computer Technician (NQF 5, 282 credits).

Delivered through an accredited provider, every module also supports your B-BBEE Skills Development scorecard — recognised training, properly documented. Your people build a portable national credential; SPS earns claimable skills spend along the way.

The accredited pathway

The Occupational Certificate: Computer Technician is the nationally accredited qualification the shorter modules ladder toward — delivered in association with Centenary Networks (Pty) Ltd, accredited by the Quality Council for Trades and Occupations (QCTO) as a Skills Development Provider.

Learners choose their modules, build credits at their own pace around their work, and earn a credential that's theirs to keep.
